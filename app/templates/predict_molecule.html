CD73 Inhibitor Bioactivity Prediction App

By Francesco D'Aleo

This app is inspired by the content featured on the YouTube channel located at https://www.youtube.com/@DataProfessor.

The purpose of this app is to predict the bioactivity of CD73 inhibitors using molecular descriptors from the PaDEL-Descriptor software. These features are then used to train a machine learning model on the ChEMBL Database. Users can upload a file containing multiple molecules.

Tumors, a disease with a high mortality rate worldwide, have become a serious threat to human health. Exonucleotide-5'-nucleotidase (CD73) is an emerging target for tumor therapy. Its inhibition can significantly reduce adenosine levels in the tumor microenvironment. In the immune response, extracellular ATP exerts immune efficacy by activating T cells. However, dead tumor cells release excess ATP, overexpress CD39 and CD73 on the cell membrane and catabolize this ATP to adenosine. This leads to further immunosuppression. There are a number of inhibitors of CD73 currently under investigation. (Zhang et al., 2023).
